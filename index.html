Chapter 01

Katse Dam

Katse Dam stands as one of Africa's most remarkable feats of engineering. Located in the Maluti Mountains of Lesotho, it is a double-curvature arch dam completed in 1996 as part of the Lesotho Highlands Water Project. Rising 185 metres above the valley floor, its arch structure elegantly transfers the immense water pressure into the surrounding rock walls. The reservoir stretches over 45 kilometres into the mountain terrain, storing water that is transferred northward to South Africa through a series of tunnels. Beyond its engineering brilliance, the dam supplies critical water to the Gauteng region while generating hydroelectric power for Lesotho, making it a cornerstone of southern African development.

Chapter 02

Katse Intake Tower

Rising from the deep blue waters of the Katse reservoir is the iconic Intake Tower — a cylindrical concrete structure that serves as the gateway through which water begins its long journey to South Africa. The tower contains a series of valves and control gates that regulate the flow of water into the transfer tunnel below. Engineers designed the tower to draw water from varying depths, allowing for quality control depending on conditions in the reservoir. It stands as both a functional marvel and a striking visual landmark, often photographed rising dramatically from the still mountain waters with the dam wall visible in the background.

Chapter 03

Mphorosane Bridge

Spanning a dramatic gorge carved by one of the reservoir's arms, the Mphorosane Bridge is a slender, elegant structure that connects communities separated by the rising waters of the Katse reservoir. Built as part of the infrastructure development accompanying the Lesotho Highlands Water Project, the bridge replaced routes that were submerged when the dam was filled. It rises high above the turquoise water below, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountain landscape. The bridge has since become a popular attraction for visitors to the dam area, admired for both its graceful engineering and the spectacular scenery it reveals.
